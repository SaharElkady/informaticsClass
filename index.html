Resilience research line

About

The Tecnun Resilience team focuses on the development of more resilient and sustainable urban environments. Its main projects have focused on the capacities of cities to cope with both foreseen and unforeseen crises arising from climate change, the interdependence of critical infrastructures, social dynamics and cyber attacks. Group model building (GMB), system dynamics, Delphi Study and focus group techniques are used for this purpose. In recent years, participation in both European and local projects has led research to focus mainly on climate change and cybersecurity issues. In Tecnun Resilience we are currently working on:

  • Social resilience analysis specifying how the interactions between authorities and emergency services with society have to be in order to involve society in disaster management and to better manage and respond to disasters.
  • Assessment and management models for resilience building in urban environments to better prepare for, respond to and recover from crises resulting from climate change, interdependence of critical infrastructures and social dynamics.
  • Development of new governance models in the urban environment in the context of climate change.
  • Frameworks for managing public-private and people partnerships to better implement resilience strategies in cities.
  • Management models to develop the awareness of the agents that make up a city to better implement resilience strategies on climate change issues.
  • Development of cyber resilience in companies to better prepare, respond and recover from cyber-attacks.
